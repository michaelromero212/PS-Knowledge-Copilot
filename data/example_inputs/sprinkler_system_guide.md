# Sprinkler & Irrigation Guide
> Source: Field Crew SOP — Irrigation

## New installations
- A typical residential system has **4 to 8 zones** depending on yard size and plant types.
- Installation usually takes **1 to 2 days**. We use a vibratory plow to minimize digging and lawn damage.
- Every new install includes a **smart Wi-Fi controller** and a one-year workmanship warranty.
- Customers should expect some minor lawn scarring along pipe lines that heals within 2 to 3 weeks.

## Spring start-up
- We turn systems on in spring, pressurize slowly to avoid pipe shock, and check every head.
- We also run a **backflow test** (required by the county once a year) and adjust each zone's run time.

## Fall winterization (blow-out)
- Before the first hard freeze we blow compressed air through each zone to clear water from the lines.
- **This is critical** — water left in pipes freezes, expands, and cracks them. Skipping it is the #1 cause of expensive spring repairs.
- Winterization is a separate scheduled visit, typically booked September through November.

## Common repairs
- **Head not spraying**: usually clogged or broken; a quick swap.
- **Zone won't turn on**: often a bad solenoid on the valve or a wiring/controller issue.
- **Soggy spot in the lawn**: likely a cracked line or leaking fitting underground.

## What to tell customers
- Recommend watering **early morning** (4–8 a.m.) to reduce evaporation and disease.
- New systems are not "set and forget" — run times should be adjusted seasonally, which our smart controller handles automatically.
