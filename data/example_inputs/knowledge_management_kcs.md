# Knowledge Management (KCS)
> Source: Google Drive — Service Delivery Shared Folder

## Why Knowledge Management Matters
A strong knowledge base deflects tickets, speeds resolution, and preserves institutional expertise. **Knowledge-Centered Service (KCS)** is the leading methodology: capture knowledge as a by-product of solving problems, not as a separate project.

## The KCS Loop
1. **Capture**: Write a knowledge article while resolving the incident, in the context of the customer's issue.
2. **Structure**: Use a consistent template so articles are scannable and reusable.
3. **Reuse**: Search for existing knowledge before troubleshooting from scratch; link the article to the ticket.
4. **Improve**: Flag articles that are outdated or unclear so they get updated ("fix at the point of use").

## Article Structure
A good knowledge article has four parts:
- **Issue / Question**: The problem in the customer's own words.
- **Environment**: Affected product, version, platform, or configuration.
- **Resolution**: Clear, numbered steps that actually fixed it.
- **Cause** (optional): Why it happened, for deeper understanding.

## Quality Signals
- Articles are **findable** (good titles and keywords), **accurate**, and **complete**.
- Track **article reuse rate** and **self-service success rate**, not just article count.
- Retire or merge duplicate articles — clutter reduces trust in the knowledge base.

## Best Practices
- Write for the reader who is stuck right now: lead with the resolution steps.
- "Just enough" content beats an exhaustive essay — capture it while it's fresh, refine it through reuse.
- Make knowledge creation part of the workflow, not an afterthought that never happens.
