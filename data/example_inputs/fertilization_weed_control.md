# Fertilization & Weed Control Program
> Source: Service Guide — Lawn Health

## The season-long program
Our standard program is **6 treatments** spread across the year, about 6 to 8 weeks apart:
1. **Early spring** — pre-emergent to stop crabgrass before it sprouts, plus light feeding.
2. **Late spring** — broadleaf weed control (dandelions, clover) and balanced fertilizer.
3. **Early summer** — slow-release fertilizer and spot weed control.
4. **Mid summer** — grub prevention and drought-tolerant feeding.
5. **Early fall** — the most important feeding for root growth heading into winter.
6. **Late fall** — "winterizer" fertilizer that strengthens roots for spring green-up.

## Safety — the question customers ask most
- After a treatment, keep **kids and pets off the lawn until it is fully dry**, usually **1 to 2 hours**.
- Once dry, the lawn is safe for normal use.
- We post a small yard sign after each visit as a reminder.

## Watering after treatment
- **Granular fertilizer**: water within 24 hours to move it into the soil.
- **Weed control (liquid)**: do NOT water for at least 24 hours — it needs time to absorb into the weed's leaves.

## Setting expectations
- Weeds don't vanish overnight. Broadleaf weeds curl and die back over **7 to 14 days**.
- A thick, healthy lawn is the best weed control — bare spots are where weeds move in.
- If weeds persist after two weeks, we offer a **free service call** to re-treat.
