# Common Customer Questions (FAQ)
> Source: Customer Service Knowledge Base

Quick answers to the questions the office and crews hear most often.

## "Why are there brown spots in my lawn?"
Common causes: dog urine, dull mower blades, grubs eating the roots, drought stress, or fungus. Ask what changed recently. Grub damage pulls up like loose carpet — that's a clue. Offer a crew visit to diagnose.

## "How often should I water?"
About **1 to 1.5 inches per week**, including rain. Better to water **deeply 2 to 3 times a week** than a little every day — deep watering grows deeper, more drought-resistant roots. Early morning is best.

## "Is the fertilizer safe for my kids and dog?"
Yes, once it's dry. Keep them off the lawn for **1 to 2 hours** after a treatment until it dries.

## "Do you offer a discount?"
Yes — prepaying the fertilization season saves **10%**, and we run referral credits. Bundling multiple services can also be quoted at a better rate.

## "Can you come today?"
We run fixed routes, so same-day isn't usually possible, but we can add them to the next available window. Never over-promise a time — offer the next real opening.

## "My sprinkler zone isn't working."
Take the address and a short description (which zone, no water vs. weak water). It's usually a head, solenoid, or controller issue. Book a repair visit; don't try to diagnose over the phone.

## "It rained — are you still coming?"
If conditions are unsafe or the ground is saturated, we reschedule to the next available day and text them. We don't mow saturated lawns because it damages the turf.

## When to escalate
- Billing disputes over $100, or any refund request → office manager.
- Property damage claims → office manager immediately; take photos, don't admit fault, don't promise payment.
- An upset customer you can't satisfy → offer the free return visit, then hand off to a supervisor.
