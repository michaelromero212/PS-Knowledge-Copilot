# Lawn Mowing Service
> Source: Field Crew SOP — Mowing

## Schedules
- **Weekly** mowing is the default during peak growing season (roughly April through October).
- **Bi-weekly** is available for slower-growing or drought-dormant lawns, usually in mid-summer or late fall.
- Crews run fixed routes by day of week, so a customer's mow day stays consistent.

## What's included in every mow
- Cutting the lawn at the proper height.
- **Edging** along walks, drives, and beds.
- **Blowing off** all hard surfaces so no clippings are left on concrete.

## Mowing height
- We mow most cool-season grasses at **3 to 3.5 inches**. Taller grass shades out weeds and holds moisture.
- We never remove more than **1/3 of the blade** in a single cut — scalping stresses the lawn and invites weeds.
- Blades are kept sharp; dull blades tear grass and leave brown, frayed tips.

## Grass clippings
- We **mulch clippings back into the lawn** by default — they return nitrogen and are good for the soil.
- Bagging and haul-away is available as a paid add-on, or when the grass is very tall or wet.

## Weather policy
- We do not mow when it's actively raining or the ground is saturated (it rips up turf and leaves ruts).
- Rained-out days push the route to the next available day; customers are notified by text.

## Common customer questions
- "Why is my lawn a different length than my neighbor's?" — different grass types and heights per customer preference.
- "Can you cut it shorter?" — we can, but explain that scalping causes weeds and browning; we recommend against going below 2.5 inches.
