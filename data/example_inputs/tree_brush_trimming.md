# Tree, Shrub & Brush Trimming
> Source: Field Crew SOP — Yard Maintenance

## What we offer
- Trimming and shaping of **shrubs and hedges**.
- Pruning of **small to medium trees** and ornamental trees.
- **Brush clearing** along fence lines and property edges.
- Cleanup and **haul-away** of all trimmings (included in the price unless the customer declines).

## What we do NOT do
- **Large tree removal**, tall-tree work requiring a bucket truck, or **stump grinding** — we refer these to our partner arborist for safety and liability reasons.
- Cutting anything near power lines — that's the utility's job; never let a crew touch it.

## Best timing
- **Shrubs/hedges**: trim in late spring after the first flush of growth, and again in mid-summer to keep shape.
- **Deciduous trees**: prune in **late winter / early dormancy** when the tree is dormant and structure is easy to see.
- **Spring-flowering shrubs** (lilac, forsythia): trim right **after** they bloom, or you cut off next year's flowers.

## Setting expectations
- We prune for plant health and shape, not a "buzz cut." Over-trimming stresses plants.
- Debris haul-away is standard. If a customer wants to keep wood chips or logs, note it on the work order.
- We can quote recurring seasonal trimming so shrubs stay tidy year-round.
