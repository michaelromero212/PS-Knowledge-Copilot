# Seeding & Aeration
> Source: Service Guide — Lawn Renovation

## Core aeration
- Aeration pulls small plugs of soil out of the lawn to relieve **compaction** and let air, water, and nutrients reach the roots.
- We recommend it **once a year**, ideally in the **fall**.
- Leave the soil plugs on the lawn — they break down on their own within a couple of weeks.

## Overseeding
- Overseeding spreads fresh grass seed over an existing lawn to fill thin spots and crowd out weeds.
- **Best time is early fall** (warm soil, cool air, fewer weeds competing). Spring is the second-best window.
- Aeration + overseeding done together is our most popular renovation — the seed falls into the aeration holes for great soil contact.

## Watering new seed — the key to success
- New seed must stay **consistently moist** until it germinates.
- Water **lightly 2 to 3 times a day** for the first 2 to 3 weeks. Short, frequent watering — not one long soak.
- Once the new grass is established, taper back to normal deeper, less frequent watering.

## What to tell customers
- **Don't mow** new seed until it reaches about **3 inches**, usually 3 to 4 weeks.
- Keep foot traffic off newly seeded areas while it establishes.
- Germination times vary by grass type but most seed sprouts in **7 to 21 days**.
- Results depend heavily on watering — if a customer skips watering, the seed won't take, and that's the most common reason for a disappointing result.
